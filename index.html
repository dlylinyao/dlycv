Motivation

When preparing for IELTS writing, I tried ChatGPT for help and the generated texts were fairly reasonable. No wonder more and more universities are employing AI text detection services to cope with students' AI cheating issues.

But, how accurate are they? I conducted an experiment using Quora question answers as Positive samples and ChatGPT generated answers as Negative samples. The mainstream platforms, Turnitin and GPT Zero, were neither accurate nor reliable, with high False Positive rates and no explanation (typical AI black boxes).

So, is it possible to utilize Linguistics to improve AI text detection? I have some novel research ideas and related questions:

- Can Linguistic features be used to distinguish AI generated and human-written texts? Such features can be lexical, semantic, syntactic, pragmatic, stylistic and cultural, and might be measured using NLP tools on both types of texts, of which the ones with statistic discriminability can be selected. But not all of them can be directly captured by existing NLP tools thus necessitating the development of new ones.
- Can Linguistic features be combined with mainstream Large Language Model (LLM) based AI text detectors? With prompt-tuning and fine-tuning, those measured Linguistic features might be injected into LLM prompt or integrated with LLM intermediate features, to augment the performance.
- Can the texts between pure AI generation and pure human writing be detected? There are cases that fall into the "gray area", such as, AI rephrased AI generation, human rephrased AI generation and AI translated human writing. To what degree can the AI part in these types of texts be detected?

To realize this, my existing knowledge in Linguistic & Translation Study should be supplemented and broadened. That is why I am applying for Master's program in Language Technology provided by the University of Helsinki.

Probably, I will work as an AI detection product manager after graduation, to fight against AI-based misconducts in both study and research.

My experience as the No. 1 employee in a high-tech startup and securing a ¥5,000,000 angel investment has demonstrated my comprehensive capabilities in business plan, negotiation and intellectual property. This will enable me to collaborate with students in related fields and transform ideas into innovative projects.
